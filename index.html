Regional climate snapshot

The region has a strong seasonal rainfall system driven by large atmospheric circulation (monsoon), and is experiencing shifts in timing and intensity that affect agriculture and water supplies. Mountain glaciers at high elevations are retreating, reducing dry-season meltwater for rivers. Coastal megacities face rising sea levels and increasing storm surge risk. Heatwaves and extreme rainfall events have become more frequent and intense in many areas.

Local implications

  • Monsoon variability leads to crop yield uncertainty and strains water storage systems.
  • More frequent heat extremes increase health risks, especially for outdoor workers and vulnerable populations.
  • Coastal erosion and flooding put infrastructure and densely populated coastal zones at risk.

Practical local responses

  • Expand renewable energy (solar, wind) and improve grid flexibility to reduce emissions.
  • Adopt water conservation measures like rainwater harvesting and recharge structures.
  • Promote climate-smart agriculture and reduce food loss to increase resilience.

Quick overview: global climate change

Global climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns driven primarily by increased greenhouse gas concentrations from human activities such as fossil-fuel combustion, deforestation and industrial processes. These changes cause warming, sea level rise, altered precipitation patterns, and more frequent extreme events.

Causes

  • Burning coal, oil and gas
  • Land-use change and deforestation
  • Industrial emissions and certain agricultural practices

Impacts

  • Sea-level rise and coastal flooding
  • More frequent heatwaves, droughts and heavy rainfall
  • Threats to biodiversity and food systems

What you can do

  • Shift to low-carbon energy and more efficient transport
  • Reduce, reuse, recycle and cut food waste
  • Support policies and investments that increase resilience and cut emissions

Simple actions you can start today

At home

Switch to LED lighting, improve insulation, and choose energy-efficient appliances.

Transport

Walk, cycle, carpool or use public transit; reduce unnecessary vehicle trips.

Food & water

Plan meals to reduce waste, favor lower-carbon foods and conserve water with simple measures.
